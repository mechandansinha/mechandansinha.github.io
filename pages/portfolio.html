Research

  • Turbulent Combustion & Spray Research Lab | Dept. of Aerospace Engineering, IISc. Bangalore (May 2019 - Present)

    Working as a Research Assistant in Propulsion & Energy Conversion Labs, Dept. of Aerospace Engineering at IISc. Bangalore under the guidance of Prof. Swetaprovo Chaudhury. I am working alongside doctoral candidate Mr. R Gopakumar where we aim to understand the blow-off dynamics of interacting swirl premixed flames in gas turbine applications.

    Premixed or partially premixed swirling flames are used due to their high ignition efficiency and low NOx emissions. Operating in lean mode, the interaction between combustor pressure oscillations and reactants' supply rates produces equivalence ratio perturbations leading to combustion instabilities. We have been trying to comprehend this phenomenon in a model combustor housing three interacting swirl flames. The system is characterized by simultaneous multi-kHz OH* Chemiluminescence and pressure measurements while stereoscopic PIV and OH-PLIF imaging are used to probe flame-flame interactions.

    I handle the data analysis of experimental data to compute critical parameters like normal, curvature, strain rate, etc. for different swirler configurations. Also worked on analytical validation of the code. I am also working on implying the machine learning algorithm to predict blow-off conditions under the supervision of Prof. Konduri Aditya from the Dept. of Computational and Data Sciences. Recently, I co-authored a research paper where we looked into the mechanism of quasi-periodic extinction-reignition (evident from low frequency, large amplitude oscillations) and the final blowoff event occurring inside the combustor.

  • Dynamic Modeling Of 3-Axis Gimbal for Smartphone Stabilization | IITH (Jan 2017 - Apr 2017)

    Since most of my videos are shot on mobile, I needed a handy solution for stabilization. Smartphones with default optical or electronic stabilization are there but their use induce jitter in the final footage.

    As an undergraduate researcher, I did my honours project on the dynamic modelling of 3-axis camera gimbal for smartphones in my senior year. I aimed to come up with a system for counterbalancing any perturbations, induced due to walking or irregular terrains, through active stabilization for better hand-held cinematography. I worked on the mechanical modeling, forward kinematics along with the dynamic characterization of the gimbal and then designed the control system in MATLAB Simulink. See my presentation here.

    Earlier, I had built Cinepad v1.0 which was basically a linear slider which allowed both translational & rotational DOF for camera movement. Shot a lot of my early YouTube through this.
